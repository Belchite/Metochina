# OSINT Community Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The Metochina community is committed to providing a welcoming, ethical, and responsible environment for all participants. We recognize that OSINT tools carry significant potential for both benefit and harm, and we hold ourselves to a high standard of ethical conduct.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Responsible Use

- Use Metochina and its capabilities only for lawful, ethical, and authorized purposes.
- Always obtain proper authorization before analyzing files or images belonging to others.
- Consider the potential impact of your analysis before acting on the results.
- Do not use OSINT techniques to circumvent security measures without explicit authorization.
- When in doubt about whether an action is ethical, err on the side of caution and do not proceed.

### 2. No Stalking, Harassment, or Doxxing

- **Stalking**: Never use metadata analysis to track, monitor, or follow any individual without their knowledge and consent.
- **Harassment**: Never use information obtained through metadata analysis to intimidate, threaten, or harass any person.
- **Doxxing**: Never publish, share, or distribute personal information (locations, home addresses, workplaces, daily routines) obtained from metadata analysis without explicit consent from the individual concerned.
- These prohibitions apply regardless of who the subject is, including public figures, and regardless of the platform or medium used for distribution.

### 3. Public Does Not Mean Without Ethical Consideration

The fact that an image or file is publicly accessible does not grant unrestricted license to analyze, aggregate, or redistribute the metadata it contains.

- Publicly available data still belongs to someone and may reveal deeply personal information.
- Aggregating metadata from multiple public sources can create detailed profiles that the individuals never intended to expose.
- The technical ability to extract information does not constitute moral permission to do so.
- Always ask: "Would the person who created this file be comfortable knowing I am analyzing it in this way?"

### 4. Respect Privacy Even When Analyzing Metadata

- Treat all personally identifiable information discovered through metadata analysis with the utmost care and discretion.
- Minimize data collection: only extract and retain the metadata you actually need for your legitimate purpose.
- Securely delete metadata and analysis results when they are no longer needed.
- Never share raw metadata findings publicly without redacting personally identifiable information.
- Be mindful that GPS coordinates, timestamps, and device identifiers can be combined to identify and locate individuals.

### 5. Report Vulnerabilities Responsibly

If your metadata analysis reveals a security vulnerability, privacy risk, or data exposure affecting an individual or organization:

- **Do not exploit the vulnerability** for personal gain or to cause harm.
- **Notify the affected party** privately and promptly, providing them with enough information to understand and address the issue.
- **Allow reasonable time** for the affected party to respond and remediate before considering any public disclosure.
- **Follow established responsible disclosure practices**, such as those outlined by FIRST or the organization's own security policy.
- **Do not publicize** the vulnerability or the personal data involved without giving the affected party adequate opportunity to respond.

## Community Standards

### Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and considerate in all community interactions.
- Provide constructive feedback and engage in good-faith discussions.
- Share knowledge and techniques responsibly, with appropriate context about ethical use.
- Credit others' work and contributions.
- Help newcomers understand both the technical and ethical dimensions of OSINT.

### Unacceptable Behavior

- Using Metochina or community resources for any illegal activity.
- Sharing techniques, scripts, or workflows specifically designed to facilitate stalking, harassment, or doxxing.
- Posting or sharing personally identifiable information obtained through metadata analysis without consent.
- Encouraging or instructing others to use OSINT tools unethically.
- Trolling, personal attacks, or derogatory comments within community spaces.
- Any form of discrimination based on race, gender, sexual orientation, disability, nationality, religion, or any other protected characteristic.

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you witness or experience a violation of this Code of Conduct, please report it by:

1. Opening a private issue on the project's GitHub repository (if the report is not sensitive)
2. Contacting the project maintainers directly via email for sensitive matters

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and confidentially.

### Consequences

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

1. **Warning**: A private written notice clarifying the violation and expected future behavior.
2. **Temporary suspension**: Temporary removal from community spaces and interaction channels.
3. **Permanent ban**: Permanent removal from all community spaces for severe or repeated violations.

The severity of the response will be proportional to the nature of the violation, with consideration given to context, intent, and prior conduct.
